Md Golam Moula Mehedi Hasan

Assistant Professor (Tenure Track) · Computer Science · Iona University

I am an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Iona University, where I have been on the tenure track since August 2022. My research focuses on the security and trustworthiness of AI systems — specifically, how explainability methods like SHAP and LIME can be exploited by adversaries, and how counterfactual explanations can be made more meaningful, fair, and robust. Before joining Iona, I spent six years as a software engineer in the financial industry and completed my Ph.D. at Tennessee Tech University.

Research

Current focus areas and active projects

XAI-Guided Adversarial Attacks
In Progress · Target: IEEE TDSC or AAAI 2027

With Sushil Poudel (Caldwell University) and Kshitiz Aryal (University of Nebraska at Omaha). Investigates how SHAP-based explanations can be systematically exploited to craft adversarial examples that evade intrusion detection systems — demonstrating that transparency and security can pull in opposite directions in deployed XAI pipelines.

Active
Counterfactual Retraining and Model Behavior Shift
In Progress · Target: JAIR or IJCAI 2027

Examines how retraining on counterfactual examples shifts a model's decision boundary, and whether that shift has implications for robustness and differential privacy guarantees.

Active
Fairness in Artificial Intelligence
NSF Proposal · With Dr. Ashiqur Rahman (Florida International University)

NSF grant proposal in active development targeting NSF CISE Core Research (IIS) and NSF CAREER programs.

Active
Causal Inference for Explainable Malware Classification
In Progress · Advancing toward submission

Applies causal discovery to identify code behaviors that directly drive malware classification decisions, producing more faithful and human-readable explanations than standard attribution methods.

Active
FSProtoTransfer: Few-Shot Prototype Transfer Learning for Fraud Detection
In Progress · Advancing toward submission

Addresses data scarcity in fraud detection by learning compact, transferable prototypes that adapt across institutions with minimal labeled examples.

Active
